## 🧭 DIRECTION NOTES

### What's clicking
- **Italian words that sound evocative and cool without needing translation** — DRAGHÌ, NOTTE, Sfizio hit differently than "here's a translated concept"
- **Italian mythological / creature names** — DRAGHÌ is the frontrunner for a reason; mysterious, strong, sounds great
- **Italian plural animal/creature names** — LUPO → Lupi direction. The "I [plural noun]" construction in Italian works as a group name
- **Insider Italian-American social terms** — The Cugz (Cugi's), The Compari. Names that feel like something you'd only *get* if you're in the culture
- **Third Coast** — the one English name that worked. Concept: diaspora creates its own place, a third identity. Works when English is *big and conceptual*, not descriptive
- **Short and moody** — NOTTE, VOLTA, DRAGHÌ. 2 syllables max. Names that live on a poster

### What doesn't work
- **[Word] Sons / [Word] Kings** — every single one rejected. Kill this pattern permanently
- **English compound nature/place words** — Storm Ridge, Gold Smoke, Split Root, etc. Too generic, too "band name generator"
- **Literal Italian heritage words** — Sangue (Blood), Radici (Roots), Filo (Thread) — too on-the-nose, try-hard, don't sound cool
- **Leet-speak stylized spellings** — BRAC3, VOLT3, BLOC3 — not landing
- **Descriptive English pairs** — Rough Grace, Wild Grace, Ghost Feast — clever but don't feel like a real group name

### Explore next
- Italian dialect words from Campania / Irpinia (not standard textbook Italian — the real vernacular)
- More Italian plural creature/animal names: Lupi, Aquile, Falchi, Serpi, Corvi...
- Italian words for darkness, mystery, the underworld — NOTTE direction
- Italian words that are phonetically appealing in English-speaking mouths — sounds cool even if you don't know Italian
- "I + [Italian plural noun]" constructions — e.g. "I Draghi" — how groups name themselves in Italian
- Possibly just a single Italian word, no article — raw, confident

### References / Inspiration
- DRAGHÌ is the north star — names in that spirit: mythological weight + short + sounds good said aloud
- Think: what would this look like on a venue marquee in NYC? On an album cover?
- Italian-American identity without being *about* Italian-American identity — it should just BE it
